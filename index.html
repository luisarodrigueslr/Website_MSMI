ABOUT

The Master in Interactive Systems and Media provides advanced and specialized training focused on animation production and systems development in ubiquitous environments. Therefore, it brings together a strong technological component with high artistic capacity, in the process of designing and creating products, experiences and systems with different degrees of interactivity. This cycle seeks to respond and explore the opportunity provided by new technologies and digital media. Provides students with theoretical and practical knowledge and skills applied to Web development and digital platforms; in the creation of interactive environments for virtual/augmented reality, for video games or interactive installations, and in the production of digital animation and motion graphics for interactive contexts, Videomapping or performance. This degree privileges partnerships with external entities, such as technology companies and creative industries.

GESTURE AND SYNTHESIS

MASTERCLASS | Diogo Tudela + José Alberto Gomes + João Tiago Dias
ESMAD | Luís Soares Auditorium | November 30th | 11:00 am
The discipline of creation or engineering involves navigating along a fissure. Such a space becomes even more apparent when logical-mathematical structures are instrumentalized in the construction (creation) of works of art. The autonomy and polarity between the disciplinary space that forms meaning, and the discipline that signifies form, produces an insoluble phenomenological gap between the vectors that point to a work and the vectors that leave it. Gesto e Síntese, which takes place and develops in a hybrid space between performance and installation, music and new media art, is thus a piece that openly works on this fissure, exploring the tooling of logical-mathematical structures under the guise of materiality and plasticity.
Gesto e Síntese is a Supernova Ensemble commission.
Supernova Ensemble is part of the Resident Artist project of the Circular.
Creation: Diogo Tudela + Supernova Ensemble | Performance: João Dias, José Alberto Gomes, Mário Costa and Diogo Tudela | Sound Design: Daniel Santos | Direction Artist: João Dias and José Alberto Gomes | Co-production: Circular Festival and gnration

Andreia Fernandes

Andreia Fernandes' project aims to apply Augmented Reality (AR) to an illustrated children's book, in order to expand and enrich it. This explored the digital component of AR and characteristics of a physical book, resulting in a printed narrative. The digital component will allow the revelation of characters that are not in the physical component.

Sara Goncalves

This project, carried out by Sara Gonçalves, was in the context of an internship for Canal180. This fits into the field of emotions, links body expression (from dance) to color, as both represent this visually. Using sensors, body movements were captured and transmitted to the computer, later using software, animations were generated for each of the emotions, resulting in 5 (happiness, anger, sadness, fear and disgust). The purpose of animation is to “separate” different programs in order to announce them and prepare the audience for the feeling that the program will convey.

Eduardo Silva

Due to the growth of tourism in cities such as Porto and Lisbon, they are the most chosen, leaving others “forgotten”, such as Trás-os-Montes. Therefore, Eduardo Silva, to help reduce this discrepancy, decided to create an interactive physical book and to complete it developed an application in Augmented Reality. This book has 5 different types of content, such as recipes, city information (ruins, churches and monuments) and nature (plants and animals). The book helps the user to explore the city and the application helps to obtain details/animations/past/among others of the place where he is, making the experience immersive. This project aims to promote tourism in this region.

Sara Gomes

More and more portuguese use e-commerce to make their purchases, but this method has led to an increase in inequalities in terms of access, especially among people with disabilities, more specifically individuals with visual impairments. From there, Sara Gomes investigated and gathered usability guidelines in order to make these platforms more inclusive. It started by investigating existing barriers in them and developing some guidelines according to the existing problems and using a visual component, it was improving them in order to combat these problems. In addition to research, she developed a book in digital format that brings together the guidelines he developed, her conclusions and visual components that allow clear sharing to designers and companies that want to expand usability and accessibility in their websites. Sara's project makes the reader aware of visual impairment by providing discussions about inclusiveness.

Jorge Pereira

The author of this project, Jorge Pereira, seeks light as a means of communication and artistic expression, through an interactive installation in which sensory issues in space are worked on due to the subjectivity of each person's visual interpretation of the project, provoking the look, imagination or/and perception of it. This work refers to themes such as the theme of light, its propagation in space-time and human interaction with light, color and form, and aims to promote ways of perceiving the physical events of light and observing its movement, arrangement and tonality, providing unique experiences for each individual.

André Macedo

Due to the moments of anxiety and nervousness that the preoperative situation provides, particularly in children, André Macedo developed a game to reveal the operating room and who works in it, using Virtual Reality. This digital component allows children to role-play with professionals in the operating room, becoming a tool for reducing nervousness and anxiety caused by fear. The project aims to understand how technology, more specifically Virtual Reality, as a means of reassuring and educating in a light and lively way.

Catarina Marinho

Catarina Marinho saw Augmented Reality as a solution to combat the negative effects that technology causes in terms of social and emotional skills, which are related to the level of children's cognition, distancing them from the real world and preventing their fundamental learning. This solution connects the digital reality with the real one, thus producing a ludic, educational and dynamic game in which the technological component makes it possible to expand the traditional physical game, in order to enhance the cognitive, motor, social and emotional abilities of children, especially the who are between 4 and 6 years old. The game establishes a relationship between letters, colors and fruits.

Jorge Barros

The Sensory Medley project aims to explore the quality of synesthesia (a neurological condition in which the affected person has two of their senses mixed, changing their perception of the world) using Virtual Reality and multimedia technologies that stimulate and relate senses and movements in a way wide and immersive. This technology allows you to enhance sensory experiences and synesthesia connections. Jorge Barros, with this project, aimed to simulate the neurological condition in the virtual world by adding the sense of smell.

Ana Silva

Ana Silva's project explores the concept of augmented storytelling in order to develop an emotional relationship between the user and the city of Porto, which led to the development of the Tell Me Porto application for tourism. This application seeks to stimulate culture based on lesser-known tales and legends, using illustrations and animations of the places it visits, making the experience different for each tourist. Ana wants the user to enjoy a different experience than he is used to and that is memorable, and at the same time correctly inform the individual of the spaces' histories.
